**The Problem**

Every productivity tool today wants to:
- Collect your email
- Store your data on their servers
- Track your behavior
- Eventually charge you

We kept asking: "Does it have to be this way?"

**Our Philosophy**

Your tasks, projects, and notes are deeply personal. You shouldn't need to trust a company with that data.

So we made Vibe Kanban local-first:
- **No servers** - Your data never leaves your browser
- **No tracking** - We literally can't see what you're organizing
- **No accounts** - Nothing to hack or breach
- **No costs** - No server costs means we can stay free forever

**The Tradeoff**

This approach means no real-time team collaboration (yet). But we learned that 80% of kanban users work solo anyway.

And the benefits? Instant performance, perfect privacy, and zero anxiety about data breaches.

## Email Best Practices

### Subject Line Testing
- Keep under 50 characters
- Use emoji sparingly (1 max)
- Ask questions
- Create curiosity
- State clear benefit

### Preview Text
- Extend the subject, don't repeat
- 40-90 characters
- Make it scannable

### Email Body
- Start with "Hi" or "Hey" (friendly, not corporate)
- Short paragraphs (2-3 lines max)
- Use emoji for visual breaks
- One primary CTA
- Mobile-friendly (60% read on mobile)

### Send Times
- **Tuesday-Thursday**: 10 AM - 2 PM (best)
- **Avoid Monday**: Inbox overload
- **Avoid Friday PM**: Weekend mode
- **Test your audience**: Everyone's different

### Frequency
- Welcome series: Immediate, Day 3, Day 7
- Feature announcements: As released (max 1/week)
- Monthly newsletter: First Tuesday of month
- Re-engagement: 30 days, 60 days, then stop

### Metrics to Track
- Open rate (aim for >25%)
- Click rate (aim for >3%)
- Reply rate (higher = better engagement)
- Unsubscribe rate (under 0.5% is good)

---

**Remember:** These emails should feel like they're from a friend, not a corporation. Be helpful, not salesy. Respect the inbox.
